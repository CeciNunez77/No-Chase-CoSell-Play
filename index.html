Services Partner Pull Co‑Sell Play

Outcome‑driven | Seller‑led | Manager‑amplified | Partner‑agnostic

SOP – How the Play Works
PBA → Partner Enablement
Exec View (Cameron / Gustavo)

1. Play Objective (Cameron's Words → Play Intent)

Primary Goal

Get Microsoft sellers and their managers to pull the Partner into Enterprise accounts, instead of the partner pushing alone.

What Success Looks Like (Example using Covenant)

  • Sellers request Covenant for AI business‑transformation conversations
  • Engagement starts at C‑suite / business leadership, not technical only
  • One proven account → expands to ~10 through seller‑manager pull

2. Play Scope & Focus

DimensionDetail

NOT security or broad services (explicitly deferred)
Customer Persona CEO / CFO / COO  •  VP Operations / VP Manufacturing
C‑suite business owners (not IT‑only)

3. Core Play Motion (End‑to‑End)

Step 1 — Customer List Intake (Partner → Microsoft)
  • Input: Partner provides updated Enterprise customer list, focused on Manufacturing + Retail
  • Purpose (explicitly stated by Cameron): Not for reporting — used as input for AI‑based segmentation and targeting inside Microsoft
Step 2 — Microsoft AI Segmentation & Targeting
  • Microsoft action: Run AI‑driven segmentation on Partner's customer list
  • Identify:
    • Best AI transformation candidates
    • Accounts with active Microsoft sellers
    • Clear industry alignment (Manufacturing first)
  • Output: Short list of high‑propensity Enterprise accounts
Step 3 — Seller + Manager Mapping (Critical — Cameron Requirement)
  • For each prioritized account, identify:
    • Account Executive (AE)
    • Seller Manager (M1)
  • Why: Cameron explicitly stated — reps alone won't move; manager‑level pull is required.
Step 4 — Anchor Customer Story (The "One Account" Rule)
  • Partner action: Select one strong Manufacturing customer
  • Prepare a simple story:
    • Business problem
    • AI transformation outcome
    • Example agents / use cases (prebuilt)
  • Rule: Start with ONE account → prove it works → expand to others
Step 5 — Seller‑Led Engagement (Pull Motion)
  • Execution: Joint meeting — Customer + Microsoft AE + Partner
  • Message:
    • "This is the problem the Partner solved"
    • "This is how AI business transformation was delivered"
  • Outcome Cameron wants:
    • AE escalates to M1
    • M1 starts pulling the Partner into additional accounts

4. Optional Enablement (If Needed)

30‑Minute Pitch Capture
  • Partner records top 3 AI business pitches
  • Microsoft translates into seller‑friendly Microsoft language
  • Purpose: Help sellers quickly understand:
    • Where the Partner fits
    • When to bring them in

6. Success Metrics (What Cameron Will Look For)

  • ✅ Sellers proactively requesting the Partner
  • ✅ Manager‑level (M1) involvement
  • ✅ Expansion from 1 → multiple Enterprise accounts
  • ✅ Tangible seller engagement (not enablement attendance)

2. How PBAs Explain This Play to the Partner

PBA Talk Track (Plain Language)

"This play is about making Microsoft sellers want to bring you into accounts because they've seen real proof that you help customers win. We start with one strong story, make sellers confident using it, and scale only when they start pulling you in."

What We Ask from the Partner

  • ONE real delivered customer outcome (not a demo or PoC).
  • Clear business problem + measurable result.
  • Willingness to improve how they talk to Microsoft sellers.

What Partners Get

  • Clear positioning with Microsoft sellers.
  • Better S2S conversations.
  • Higher‑quality introductions (not random referrals).

3. Executive View (Cameron / Gustavo)

Why This Play Exists (15‑second framing)

We reduced partner noise and PBA effort by shifting from activity‑driven co‑sell (volume, decks, campaigns) to seller‑pull driven services co‑sell, where Microsoft sellers actively request partners based on proven customer outcomes.

Why this matters:
  • Cameron repeatedly pushes back on activity without pull — "I don't care about volume, I care about outcomes"
  • This frames the play as a control mechanism, not another program

🚫 What This Play Intentionally Stopped

  • ❌ Chasing sellers without proof
  • ❌ Measuring success by # of S2S calls alone
  • ❌ Enablement decks without customer outcomes
  • ❌ Scaling partners before one anchor win

Aligns with Cameron's repeated frustration with "enablement theater" and volume metrics.
